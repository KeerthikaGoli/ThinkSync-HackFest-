Intellectual Property (IP) Rights Policy

1. Introduction

This Intellectual Property (IP) Rights Policy outlines the terms and conditions governing the ownership, use, and protection of intellectual property created during the ThinkSync Hackathon ("the Event"). All participants must carefully review and agree to these terms before participating.

2. Ownership of IP

Participants retain full ownership of the intellectual property rights to their original work created during the hackathon, including but not limited to:

  • • Source code and software applications
  • • Designs, graphics, and user interfaces
  • • Algorithms and technical solutions
  • • Documentation and presentations

3. Pre-Existing IP

Any pre-existing intellectual property used in the project must be:

  • ✓ Clearly disclosed during project submission
  • ✓ Used with proper licenses and permissions
  • ✓ Properly attributed to original creators

4. Third-Party Resources

Participants may use third-party resources (APIs, libraries, frameworks, etc.) provided they:

  • • Comply with respective license agreements
  • • Provide proper attribution and documentation
  • • Do not violate any third-party IP rights

5. License Grant to Organizers

Participants grant the Event organizers a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to:

  • • Showcase projects for promotional purposes
  • • Share project details on event websites and materials
  • • Document and archive submissions for event records

6. Confidentiality

Participants must maintain confidentiality of any proprietary information shared by organizers, sponsors, or other participants during the Event. This includes but is not limited to technical information, business strategies, and unreleased products or features.

7. IP Infringement

Participants must not infringe upon any third-party intellectual property rights. Any IP infringement may result in:

  • ❌ Immediate disqualification from the Event
  • ❌ Forfeiture of any prizes or awards
  • ❌ Potential legal consequences

8. Commercial Rights

Participants retain the right to:

  • ✓ Commercialize their projects
  • ✓ File patents or other IP protections
  • ✓ License their work to third parties

9. Publication Rights

The Event organizers reserve the right to:

  • • Photograph and record the Event
  • • Publish project descriptions and results
  • • Share participant names and project details

10. Dispute Resolution

Any disputes regarding intellectual property rights will be:

  • • Initially addressed through mediation with Event organizers
  • • Resolved according to applicable intellectual property laws
  • • Subject to binding arbitration if necessary

11. Policy Updates

The Event organizers reserve the right to update this IP Rights Policy at any time. Participants will be notified of any changes, and continued participation in the Event constitutes acceptance of such changes.
